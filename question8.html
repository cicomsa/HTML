CSS: FlexBox

The Flexible Box Module, usually referred to as flexbox, was designed as a one-dimensional layout model and method that could offer space distribution between items in an interface and with powerful alignment capabilities.

You need to think in containers and items:

The main idea behind the flex layout is to give the container the ability to alter its items' width, height and order to best fill the available space (mostly to accommodate to all kind of display devices and screen sizes). A flex container expands items to fill available free space, or shrinks them to prevent overflow.

There are many options to be explored here, more information can be found on css-tricks.com or in this medium post.
